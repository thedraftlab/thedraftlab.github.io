About

Rounak Saha Niloy is a PhD student at UNSW Sydney, working in close collaboration with the ARC Training Centre for Automated Manufacture of Advanced Composites (AMAC). He is also a co-founder of the DRAFT Lab, where his research bridges data-driven design and naval architecture.

Rounak Niloy’s research sits at the intersection of computational design, intelligent manufacturing, and naval architecture. He focuses on automating the design and fabrication of composite structures using data-driven approaches that integrate machine learning, CFD, and evolutionary algorithms. His work addresses complex multi-objective and multi-concept optimization challenges, with applications in high-performance marine and industrial systems. By combining domain expertise in naval architecture with advanced computational tools-such as surrogate modelling and physics-informed AI—Rounak aims to streamline early-stage design workflows and develop scalable, automated manufacturing pipelines that enhance both performance and efficiency.
